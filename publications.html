Causal Compression and World Model for Reverse Stress Test: a study in reduced dimension of idiosyncratic and systemic factor for Asset Liability Management

Hugo NGUYEN

Ecole Polytechnique, Institut Polytechnique de Paris, 2028

This thesis project aims to develop causal compression for the construction of a world model of the macro-financial system, with a view to a methodological overhaul of bank Reverse Stress Testing (RST). The objective is to learn a world model capable of simulating plausible extreme trajectories in an economic environment composed of numerous risk factors, while ensuring the statistical and structural consistency of the generated scenarios. The project relies on statistical learning tools applied to heterogeneous time series, including rare events. It explores in a modular way the impact of systemic factors (macroeconomics, monetary policies), idiosyncratic factors (client behaviors, sectoral exposures), and their interactions on the performance of ALM portfolios. This framework aims to enhance the explanatory and predictive power of RST by integrating a causal representation and idiosyncratic variables that are often overlooked in favor of systemic factors. The proposed methodology also seeks to ensure the inferential quality of the generated scenarios through the use of rectifiers.
